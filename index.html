Reading trade agreements against the trade data they are supposed to govern.

I work at the intersection of customs valuation, transfer pricing, and preferential origin. Over fifteen years inside export-manufacturing operations in Central America, I have managed suspensive customs regimes, defended customs valuation audits, and applied free trade agreements where the difference between the cheerful version of an FTA and the literal text of its origin rules is measured in duty exposure. 01Areas of Practice
  • Customs valuation and transfer pricing. Where related-party pricing meets the transaction value method: royalties, assists, price adjustments, and the documentary position an importer needs when the valuation question arrives after clearance.
  • FTA rules of origin. CAFTA-DR, the EU–Central America Association Agreement, and the Central America–Chile framework: qualification analysis, accumulation mechanics, certificate management, and origin verification defense.
  • Suspensive customs regimes. Temporary admission for inward processing: inventory accuracy, yield reporting, and the reconciliation discipline that keeps a regime authorization defensible year after year.
  • Customs audit defense. Technical defense of post-clearance audits, from document production strategy through settlement. I have led valuation audits from initial assessment to resolutions well below opening exposure.
  • AEO certification. Authorized Economic Operator programs under the WCO SAFE Framework: gap assessment, certification, and the operational controls behind the status.

03About

I am a supply chain and global trade compliance executive based in Newport News, Virginia, with more than fifteen years of experience across export-manufacturing and cross-border operations in Latin America. The core of that experience is compliance work done from inside the operation: managing suspensive customs regimes and their inventory reconciliation, applying free trade agreement preferences and defending them under verification, and leading the technical defense of customs valuation audits through to settlement.

I hold an Associate degree in Authorized Economic Operator programs from the World Customs Organization, a diploma in Foreign Trade and Customs Operations from SIECA, and a degree in Business Administration with a focus on international banking, finance, and marketing. I currently advise general management at a transportation company on tendering, cost governance, and digitalization. I am an active member of the International Compliance Professionals Association (ICPA). I write in English and work in Spanish.

The writing on this site follows one method: take the literal text of an agreement or a regulation, hold it against the trade data it is supposed to govern, and report what the comparison shows. Where the two diverge, there is usually a compliance exposure that nobody has audited yet.
